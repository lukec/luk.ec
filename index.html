Hi there, I'm Luke Closs.

I'm a Vancouver, BC based software developer, open data hacker and father.

I like to practice innovation, create new things and mash up old things.

I tweet, write lots of code and occaisionally create interesting videos.

A little bit about me:

I graduated in 2001 from BCIT with honours in Data Communications and Internetworking. Lesson: Learning how to learn.

From 2001 to 2004 I worked for Telos Technologies building 2g and 3g cellular soft switches. Lesson: "Things take as long as they take. Make them right."

From 2004 to 2006 I worked for Sophos on a gateway anti-spam product. I became an advocate of agile software development and having a reflective software development practice. Lesson: Be ambitious.

In 2006 I joined Socialtext's entirely distributed development team and we built a platform for social enterprise collaboration. Lesson: RESTful services are wonderful

In 2008 I helped to found the Vancouver Hack Space, one of Canada's first Hackerspaces. Lesson: You can learn a surprising amount by hanging out with the right people.

In 2009 I created VanTrash, one of the first civic open data apps in Canada. In 2011, we launched Recollect.net, an open data business with a goal to help municipalities communicate with citizens about waste collection. Lesson: small hacks can become valuable with time, strategy and attention.

In 2011 I started Closs Consulting LTD and worked on several projects to help local non-profits in the Community Scale Biodiesel and Environmental activism areas.

In 2011 I also joined Prime Radiant as the Lead Developer of Checkmarkable, an app to help organizations have repeatable, high quality processes.
